KERALA

Kerala called Keralam in Malayalam is a state on the Malabar Coast of India.[15] It was formed on 1 November 1956. It is bordered by Karnataka to the north and northeast, Tamil Nadu to the east and south, and the Lakshadweep Sea to the west. Kerala is the 13th-largest Indian state by population. It is divided into 14 districts with the capital being Thiruvananthapuram. Malayalam is the most widely spoken language and is also the official language of the state.

Malayalam is the official language of Kerala. The state has a rich cultural heritage, known for its traditional dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, classical music, and vibrant festivals. Kerala is a popular tourist destination, often referred to as "God's Own Country" due to its natural beauty. The state is a leading producer of spices, rubber, coconut, and tea. The tourism industry also plays a vital role in the economy.

Kerala is known for:

  • Backwaters: Kerala's backwaters, a network of interconnected canals, rivers, and lakes, are a major attraction.
  • Ayurveda: Kerala is a global hub for Ayurveda, an ancient system of medicine that originated in India.
  • Cuisine: Kerala's cuisine is distinct and flavorful, characterized by the extensive use of coconut, spices, and seafood.
  • Festivals: Kerala is known for its vibrant festivals, which reflect the state's rich cultural heritage.
    The most famous festival is Onam, a harvest festival celebrated with grand feasts, traditional dances, and boat races.

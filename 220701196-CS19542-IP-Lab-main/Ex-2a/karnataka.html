KARNATAKA

Karnataka is a state in the southwestern region of India. It was formed as Mysore State on 1 November 1956, with the passage of the States Reorganisation Act, and renamed Karnataka in 1973. The state is bordered by the Lakshadweep Sea to the west, Goa to the northwest, Maharashtra to the north, Telangana to the northeast, Andhra Pradesh to the east, Tamil Nadu to the southeast, and Kerala to the southwest.

Bangalore is the capital and the largest city of Karnataka. It is a major IT hub and is often referred to as the "Silicon Valley of India." Other important cities include Mysore, known for its palaces and cultural heritage; Mangalore, a key port city; and Hubballi-Dharwad, a major commercial center. Kannada is the official language of Karnataka. The state has a rich cultural heritage, with a history of various dynasties, such as the Chalukyas, Hoysalas, and Vijayanagara Empire, contributing to its art, architecture, and literature. Karnataka is known for classical dance forms like Bharatanatyam and Yakshagana, a traditional theater form.
